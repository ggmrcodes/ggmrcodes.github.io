Innovation

High-level explanation (non-proprietary) for customers, judges, and partners.

What we make

A resilient bio-composite “coffee leather” sheet with tunable thickness, texture, and finish. It is designed to mimic the hand-feel and aesthetics of animal leather while supporting lower-impact procurement.

Naturally tinted Custom texture Flexible Wear-resistant

How it works (high level)

Spent coffee grounds are cleaned, dried, and processed into a consistent particulate feedstock. The feedstock is then integrated with natural rubber and bio-based binders to form a composite matrix, cured into sheets, and finished for targeted use cases.

  • Input standardization for consistent batches
  • Composite formulation for strength + flexibility
  • Sheet forming + curing + finishing

Material performance targets

Grounded focuses on meeting procurement standards where sustainable materials often fail: mechanical performance, surface durability, and consistent quality at scale.

  • Tensile strength: 13.25 MPa (prototype result)
  • Flex endurance + abrasion durability (in-progress validation)
  • Water resistance + stain resistance (application-specific finishes)

Impact

Why stakeholders care: emissions, waste reduction, and compliance-ready procurement.

Waste-to-value system

Coffee grounds are typically treated as disposal waste. Grounded turns them into an industrial input, lowering disposal burden while creating new economic value through structured collection programs.

  • Reduces landfill-bound organic waste
  • Creates measurable ESG outcomes for partners
  • Turns waste streams into revenue streams

Compliance and ESG credibility

Customers benefit from lower-carbon materials that support sustainability disclosures, audit readiness, and eligibility for sustainability-linked procurement programs.

  • Lower-carbon material inputs (Scope 3 narrative support)
  • Reduced material toxicity vs conventional tanning
  • Traceable sourcing via partner collection network

Rubber farmer upside

Natural rubber input creates an additional demand pathway beyond commodity cycles, supporting more stable procurement relationships and value-added markets.

  • Demand diversification beyond tires / gloves
  • Potential for longer-term supply agreements
  • Value uplift through traceability and ESG-linked inputs

Validation & Progress

Evidence that the material works and the supply model is feasible.

Technical validation

We iterated formulations to optimize composite ratios and sheet forming for consistent prototypes. Current prototype tensile testing achieved 13.25 MPa, comparable to animal leather ranges reported in literature.

  • Prototype sheet production (multiple iterations)
  • Tensile testing completed; durability tests in progress
  • Finish / texture prototyping for target use cases

Supply validation

We engaged coffee-generating partners to confirm consistent availability of grounds and willingness to participate in structured collection and incentive programs.

  • Partner sourcing conversations (hotels / cafés / restaurants)
  • Collection workflow designed (storage, pickup, preprocessing)
  • Next: pilot collection + batch consistency evaluation
